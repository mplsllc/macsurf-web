MacSurf line-breaker — trouble spots (closer look)

1. hyphens — the manual vs none mystery

1a. CONTROL — plain long word, NO soft hyphens (hyphens: manual)
Expected: no break opportunities at all → the word overflows past the right border. Baseline for "what a non-breaking word looks like".
supercalifragilisticexpialidocious
1b. hyphens: manual — SAME word but with &shy; between each syllable
Expected: wraps at a soft hyphen, ONE "-" at the line end only; the shy points that do NOT wrap stay invisible. NOT a "-" between every syllable.
super­cali­fragi­listic­expiali­docious
1c. hyphens: none — identical &shy; word
Expected: soft hyphens create NO break → the word overflows the border exactly like 1a, and shows NO "-" anywhere. Must look DIFFERENT from 1b.
super­cali­fragi­listic­expiali­docious
1d. hyphens: manual — exactly ONE soft hyphen in the middle
Expected: breaks at the single shy → "aaaaaaaaaa-" on line 1, "bbbbbbbbbb" on line 2. One hyphen, one break.
aaaaaaaaaa­bbbbbbbbbb
1e. hyphens: none — same single-shy word
Expected: no break → "aaaaaaaaaabbbbbbbbbb" overflows the border, no "-".
aaaaaaaaaa­bbbbbbbbbb

2. soft hyphen at a boundary (phantom "-", issue #275)

2a. shy at a styled-inline boundary: <b>ab&shy;</b>cd (wide, no wrap)
Expected: "abcd" — the shy is mid-word, no wrap here, so it stays invisible. Bug: a stray "-" appears ("ab-cd").
ab­cd
2b. shy immediately before a forced break: foo&shy;<br>bar
Expected: "foo" / "bar" — the <br> is the break, not the shy, so no "-". Bug: "foo-" before the break.
foo­
bar

3. justify — line before a forced <br>

3a. justified block whose first paragraph ends at a <br>
Expected (CSS Text L3): the line right before the <br> is start/left-aligned, NOT stretched. So "The quick brown fox jumps" should be naturally spaced. Bug: it gets spread edge-to-edge.
The quick brown fox jumps
then trots home slowly to rest.
3b. CONTROL — same text justified with NO <br>
Expected: interior lines stretched to the border; only the true LAST line ("rest.") left-aligned.
The quick brown fox jumps then trots home slowly to rest.
